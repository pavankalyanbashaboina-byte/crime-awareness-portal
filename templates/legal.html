Legal & Court Actions

Understanding what happens when misconduct cases escalate to the legal system

📋

Step 1: Internal Complaint

The first step is filing a complaint with the college's internal committee (ICC, Anti-Ragging Squad, or Disciplinary Committee). They investigate and attempt resolution within the institution.

Internal Level
🏛️

Step 2: Police FIR

If the matter is serious or unresolved internally, a First Information Report (FIR) can be filed at the local police station. This officially enters the legal system.

Police Level
👨‍⚖️

Step 3: Magistrate Court

Minor offences are tried in Magistrate courts. The accused is summoned, evidence is examined, and a verdict is delivered. Penalties include fines or short imprisonment.

Court Level
⚖️

Step 4: Sessions Court

Serious crimes like sexual harassment, assault, or major fraud are tried in Sessions Court. Punishments can include long-term imprisonment up to life sentence.

Serious Offences

🛡️ Rights of the Victim

Right to File FIR

Every victim has the right to file an FIR at any police station free of cost. Police cannot refuse to register an FIR for cognizable offences.

Right to Confidentiality

The identity of the victim, especially in sexual harassment cases, must be kept confidential by law under IPC Section 228A.

Right to Legal Aid

Victims who cannot afford a lawyer can get free legal aid through the District Legal Services Authority (DLSA).

Right to Compensation

Courts can order the accused to pay compensation to the victim under Section 357 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
